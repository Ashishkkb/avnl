Information Architecture (IA) Structure 
As per the DBIM ToolKit, the website must transition to a global menu-based navigation system with five mandatory Level-1 (L1) sections. The current AVNL website content will be mapped into this new architecture as follows:
Ministry/Department (AVNL Corporate):
Level 2 Content:
About Us (History, Corporate Governance, Vision & Mission)
Leadership (Board of Directors)
Our Units (HVF, EFA, OFMK, VFJ, MTPF)
Chart (Organogram)
Milestones
Map
Directory
Our Performance
Requirement:
The 'About Us' section must list objectives and functions as a bulleted list.
The 'Our Team' page must display the correct organizational hierarchy.
Offerings:
Level 2 Content:
Products & Services (Tanks, Fuzes, Engines, etc.)
Training & Development (AVNL IOLs)
Tenders
Vacancies/Careers
MSME Initiatives
Make in India
Awards & Achievements
CSR Activities
Requirement:
Only active offerings and tenders should be displayed.
Documents:
Level 2 Content:
Annual Reports
Annual Returns
Acts & Policies
Rajbhasha (Niyam Pustak, Avani Pravah)
Orders and Notices
Publications
Requirement:
Documents must be versioned with their release dates.
Titles must not exceed 250 characters.
Media:
Level 2 Content:
Photo Gallery
Video Gallery
Brochures
Presentations
Requirement:
Photos must be maintained as an album tagged with captions and dates.
Videos must display upload date, duration, and feature captions.
Connect:
Level 2 Content:
Contact Us
RTI
Vigilance (PIDPI, Whistle Blower Policy)
Public Grievances
Online Vendor Registration Application (OVRA)
Citizen Engagement


CMS Workflow and Access Role Architecture

Workflow Protocols:
Content accuracy ensured via DBIM-mandated multi-level CMS workflows.
Minimum of two tiers of approval required prior to publication.
Defined functional roles for content creation and authorized deployment. (for example, a "Creator" who drafts the content and an "Approver/Publisher" who reviews and authorizes it to go live).
Oversight & Management:
Designation of Chief Information Officer (CIO) for strategic oversight.
Web Information Manager responsible for governing the corporate digital footprint and website communications.
